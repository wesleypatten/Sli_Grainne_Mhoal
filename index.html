Welcome

The Slí Gráinne Mhaol is a scenic walking trail on Achill Island, County Mayo, Ireland, named after the legendary pirate queen Gráinne Mhaol (Grace O’Malley). This route follows the rugged coastline and dramatic cliffs, offering breathtaking views of Clew Bay, the Atlantic Ocean, and the surrounding mountains. The trail is steeped in history, passing through sites linked to Gráinne Mhaol’s seafaring exploits and the island’s rich cultural heritage. Walkers can explore remnants of ancient settlements, traditional stone walls, and wild landscapes that have remained largely unchanged for centuries. Ideal for nature lovers and history enthusiasts alike, the Slí Gráinne Mhaol provides a unique way to experience Achill’s untamed beauty and deep connection to Ireland’s past.
